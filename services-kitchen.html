Adding a Basement Kitchen

The benefits of adding a kitchen or kitchenette in your basement are significant, with value and convenience high on the list. You can double your capacity for cooking and storage while improving the value of your home.

Whether your downstairs space includes a game room, guest suite, man cave, or lady haven, having a kitchen close at hand adds convenience and comfort. Of course, you could also make a tricked-out designer kitchen the focal point of your basement remodeling project.

Installing a downstairs kitchen is also cost-effective, as it typically doesn’t require making any structural modifications. And since all the necessary utilities should be close at hand, the cost for plumbing, wiring, and drains will be substantially less than they might be for an addition or major remodeling project.

Adding a Basement Bar

Adding a bar brings fun and function to your basement. Gather with friends and family to watch a game or movie, have a girls’ or boys’ night in, or celebrate a special occasion with all your favorite beverages right at your fingertips.

Alone or in conjunction with a kitchen or kitchenette, a basement bar is the perfect addition to your game room, home theater, living room, or guest suite. In fact, with the right design — including a refrigerator, sink, and storage — your wet bar can double as a kitchenette. This increases your home’s value even further and frees up space for more storage or other amenities.
